Projects

Study of a Mira Variable Star in the Field of NGC 559

I was selected by the Indian Academy of Sciences as a Summer Research Fellow to work on this project with Dr. Yogesh Chandra Joshi. In this project, I did a photometric analysis of a Mira variable star found in the field of an intermediate-age open cluster NGC 559. I used the time-series photometric data from different telescopes, such as Gaia, ASAS-SN, WASP, ZTF, etc, in different bands.

From my analysis, I could find out the period of its variation with good accuracy. Using some standard relations involving the period, I could deduce some of the parameters of the star, such as its age and distance. The age, found from my analysis, supports the star’s classification as a Mira variable, and along with the distance, it tells us that this star is not a member of NGC 559. Moreover, a periodic pattern was found to be modulated over the purely sinusoidal brightness variations. These patterns are characteristic of Mira variables. However, I could not comment on the exact reason behind this feature. Some further analysis (especially spectroscopic analysis) may provide some more insights.

Estimating the Precession of Mercury's Orbit

The primary aim of this project was to study classical mechanics and get an idea of how we can get equations of the Keplerian orbits under Newtonian gravity. This has helped me gain insights into the conservation laws of classical mechanics, which play an important role in determining planetary motions. I also learned to solve differential equations commonly encountered in this topic.

In the later part of the project, I solved the general relativistic orbital equation using a perturbative method to get the precessing orbit of planets and the expression for the rate of precession (which is the most prominent for Mercury). I did not study general relativity before getting the general relativistic orbital equation but took the equation, which is the same as that in the Newtonian case, except for an extra term, as given in books. This helped me learn the perturbative method for solving differential equations.

Presentations

I gave a presentation on the topic Quasar Absorption Lines at the Inter-University Centre for Astronomy and Astrophysics (IUCAA) as a part of Introductory Summer School in Astronomy and Astrophysics (ISSAA), 2025. In this short presentation, I tried to give an overview of the field. I gave an introductory talk on Photometry in Astronomy on behalf of the NISER Astronomy Club. The aim of the talk was to introduce the beginners of the field with to the importance of photometry in astronomy and different photometric techniques relavant to astronomy.
